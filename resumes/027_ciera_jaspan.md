## About
About

Software engineer and researcher. I co-lead and founded the Engineering Productivity Research team at Google. We are a team of 15 software engineers and user experience researchers who seek to understand what makes developers happy and productive. We study everything about the experience of a software developer, including the impact of build latency, what makes for "high quality code",  racial/gender bias in our processes, and the effects of hybrid work. We make heavy use of mixed-methods research and draw upon research methods from a variety of fields. When possible, we publish our research in top conferences in the field, and you can find summaries of our research in a regular IEEE Software column published by myself and my co-lead, Collin Green.

Previously, I worked on static analysis and software design. My dissertation work is a specification system and static analysis to describe and detect violations of complex protocols between software frameworks and their plugins. I'm particularly interested in cost-effective tooling that improves system design. I'm also interested in software engineering education beyond basic programming skills. My research focus is on mastery of software design and architecture within the limited timeframe of an undergraduate curriculum.
